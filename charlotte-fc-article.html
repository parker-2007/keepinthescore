The Charlotte FC Effect: The Rise of Professional Soccer in the Queen City

Written by Ryan Sconzo • Feature • MLS

When Charlotte FC debuted in Major League Soccer in 2022, expectations soared. Owned by billionaire David Tepper and playing at Bank of America Stadium, the club aimed to establish professional soccer as a major force in the Carolinas. In only a few seasons, Charlotte FC has transformed from an expansion franchise into one of the most recognizable and well-supported teams in MLS.

Charlotte FC made history before its first match. The club sold more than 30,000 season tickets before its inaugural season, among the strongest launches in MLS history. That momentum continued into the March 2022 home opener, where 74,479 fans set an MLS attendance record. The turnout proved analysts wrong: Charlotte was ready for professional soccer. Long defined by NFL and NBA fandom, it quickly embraced MLS culture, creating a vibrant supporter environment rarely seen for an expansion club.

MLS has experienced steady growth, surpassing 11 million fans during the 2024 season with an average attendance of more than 23,000 per match. Charlotte FC ranks among league leaders, regularly drawing 35,000 fans per game, figures rivaling European clubs and topping most North American markets.

Charlotte FC has cultivated a new sports identity. Supporter groups like Mint City Collective and Blue Furia introduced chants, tifos, and marches mirroring global traditions. Match days extend beyond the stadium, filling uptown businesses with fans in blue and black.

Some traditions were born by accident. During the inaugural game, the microphone cut out as the national anthem ended. In a spontaneous moment, 74,000 fans finished the anthem in unison, creating a ritual unique to Charlotte.

Others were imported. The 'Poznan' — where fans turn their backs to the field and jump in rhythm — began as a nod to the club’s first two Polish players. What started as a tribute evolved into a signature pregame ritual, one that the club performs with such precision that MLS social media often highlights it as the league's best.

The team mirrors soccer’s rise across the U.S. MLS expanded aggressively over the last decade, adding franchises and investing in stadiums, academies, and talent. Charlotte’s success reinforced the league’s belief that large cities can sustain soccer communities.

While early seasons often involve rebuilding, Charlotte FC became competitive quickly. The team rode the momentum of a nine-game winning streak in 2025, the longest in the league that year, solidifying its status as a contender. After missing the playoffs in 2022, the club qualified for the MLS Cup Playoffs in 2023, a milestone for the young club. The postseason validated the investment and proved the team could compete.

The roster blends international veterans with emerging domestic talent. Midfielder Brandt Bronico leads the team with 157 appearances, anchoring the midfield alongside goalkeeper Kristijan Kahlina, who has 149 starts. On the attack, Karol Swiderski leads all scorers with 32 goals, followed by Kerwin Vargas and Patrick Agyemang, who have 22 each.

Years after kickoff, Charlotte FC reshaped the city’s sports landscape. Record crowds, culture, and success positioned the club as an MLS flagship. An ambitious expansion project evolved into defining Charlotte’s identity. As MLS rises nationally, Charlotte FC proves soccer took root in the Queen City, not as a novelty, but as a permanent tradition.
